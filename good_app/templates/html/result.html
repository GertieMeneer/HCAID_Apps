How to Know if You Ate a Bad Mushroom:

  • Symptoms of mushroom poisoning can appear from 20 minutes up to 24 hours after eating.
  • Watch for nausea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, diarrhea, sweating, dizziness, or confusion.
  • In severe cases, symptoms may include jaundice (yellow skin), seizures, or difficulty breathing.
  • If you suspect poisoning, seek medical help immediately and keep a sample of the mushroom for identification.

What to Do if You Eat a Bad Mushroom:

  • Call your local poison control center or emergency services right away.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by medical professionals.
  • Keep any uneaten mushrooms and food samples for testing.
  • Drink small sips of water to stay hydrated, but avoid alcohol or other substances.
  • Get medical help immediately, early treatment greatly improves outcomes.

How to Measure Mushrooms Correctly:

  • Use a ruler or caliper to measure the cap diameter, stem length, and thickness.
  • Take photos from multiple angles to capture identifying features.
  • Take a good look at the color and structure of the gills, spores, and texture if possible.
